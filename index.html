Water the secret to staying Healthy

It has been proven that depending on how old you are the quantity of water you drink can have a direct effect effect on your health. Drink more water

Water, the most important Solvent in your boady.

Your Water intake must and nedds to be well regulated, depending on your agitated age, body weight and size

    Children

  • 0-6 months: Around 26 ounces or 0.8 liters per day from formula or breastmilk. Starting at around 6 months, offer another 0-4 ounces of water per day. Babies 7-12 months: Around 28 ounces or 0.8-1 liters per day. Most of this can come from formula, breastmilk, and pureed foods with a bit of water.
  • Toddlers 1-2 years: Around 30 ounces or 0.9-1 liters per day. Give water routinely and encourage sips from a cup. Juice intake should be limited. Children 3-8 years: Girls-56 oz (1.6 liters), boys -70 oz (2 liters) per day. Offer water with meals and spaced throughout the day.

    • Adults

  • Adults 19-50 years: Women-11 cups (2.7 liters), Men - 15.5 cups (3.7 liters) total from water and other drinks daily. Seniors 51+ years: Their lower kidney function can lead to dehydration. Men-13 cups (3 liters), Women-11 cups (2.7 liters) daily from various sources.
